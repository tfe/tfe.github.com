I’m a startup software engineer. I love building useful things that help people solve problems. I value simplicity and clarity in design, good user experience, and quality products.

Currently I am available for consulting work in San Francisco or remotely (feel free to get in touch). I've worked with a number of high-profile, venture-backed startups doing full-stack web application development in Ruby and Javascript. Before that, I cofounded Duostack (a Ruby/Node.js hosting platform), which was acquired by Docker (then dotCloud) in May 2011.

I’m a 2008 graduate of Carnegie Mellon University where I studied information systems, statistics, and sleep deprivation. I also hold a master’s degree from the Heinz College at CMU in Information Systems Management.

When I’m not working, I like to fly airplanes and build high-precision electro-mechanical systems, and fly those too. I also enjoy travel hacking, double doubles from In-N-Out, cats, and Oxford commas.

If you’d like to talk about any of those things, don’t hesitate to get in touch.
