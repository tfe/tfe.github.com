About Me

I’m a startup software engineer. I love building useful things that help people solve their problems. I value simplicity and clarity in design, good user experience, and quality products.

Currently I am available for freelance work in San Francisco (feel free to get in touch). In the past, I've worked at Leap Motion and TaskRabbit. Before that, I cofounded Duostack (a Ruby/Node.js hosting platform), which was acquired by dotCloud in May 2011.

I’m a 2008 graduate of Carnegie Mellon University where I studied information systems, statistics, and sleep deprivation. I also hold a master’s degree from the Heinz College at CMU in Information Systems Management.

While at CMU, I transformed the fledgling student-run campus television station into one of the most prominent and fastest-growing student organizations on campus. After interning at Boeing in Seattle, I joined a young startup as co-founder and technical lead. This is where I started developing my Ruby and Javascript expertise.

When I’m not working, I like to fly airplanes and build high-precision electro-mechanical systems, and fly those too. I also enjoy clever hacks (for good, not evil), lolcats, and double doubles from In-N-Out.

If you’d like to talk about any of those things, don’t hesitate to get in touch.
